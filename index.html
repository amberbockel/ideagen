What's the toothbrush test?

Larry Page used to ask one question about every product idea at Google: would people use it twice a day, like a toothbrush? Not once a week. Not when something breaks. Twice a day, by habit. Most ideas fail that test — and that's actually useful information.

How to use this

Spin the four reels to generate a product concept — a person, a skill, a device, a problem. Lock any reel you like, spin the rest. When something clicks, copy the sentence and bring it to Claude or your next brainstorm. The question to ask: would this person reach for it every single day?

Why I built it

I think about product ideas constantly, but most of them are solutions looking for a problem. This tool forces specificity — a real person, a real context, a real friction point. The combinations are often strange and sometimes obvious, but occasionally one lands and you can't stop thinking about it. That's the one worth building.
